# Incident runbook steps

Step-by-step resolution guides for each incident type. Follow these in order; adapt based on user context.

---

## 1. Field / scheduling incident

### Technician no-show

1. Pull the work order from the portal to confirm the scheduled technician and job site details.
2. Attempt to reach the technician via their contact info on the work order.
3. If unreachable after 15 minutes, flag the work order as needing reassignment.
4. Check the portal for available technicians in the area (use the planned technicians / day schedule tool).
5. Reassign the work order to an available technician and notify them.
6. Contact the client to provide an updated ETA and apologize for the delay.
7. Update the work order notes with a summary of what happened and the resolution.
8. Log the technician's no-show for follow-up by the operations manager.

### Mis-assigned work order

1. Pull the work order and confirm the error (wrong technician, wrong location, wrong service type).
2. Identify the correct assignment based on the job requirements and technician qualifications.
3. Update the assignment in SIMPL (or flag it to the appropriate manager if you don't have edit access).
4. Notify both the incorrectly assigned technician (removed) and the correctly assigned technician (new assignment).
5. If the job is time-sensitive, confirm the correct technician can still make the scheduled time.
6. Update the work order notes with a log of the correction.

### Urgent reschedule

1. Confirm the reason for the reschedule (client request, technician unavailability, weather, etc.).
2. Check available scheduling slots in the portal for a replacement time.
3. Confirm the new time with the client and the assigned technician.
4. Update the work order in SIMPL with the new date/time.
5. Send confirmation to all parties.
6. Note the reschedule reason and new details in the work order notes.

## 2. Billing / payment error

### Failed payment

1. Pull the invoice from the portal and confirm the amount, due date, and payment method on file.
2. Check if the payment failure was due to an expired card, insufficient funds, or a processing error.
3. Contact the client with a clear description of the issue and request they update their payment method or retry.
4. If the payment method was valid and it was a processing error, retry the payment after a short wait.
5. Log the failed payment attempt on the invoice record.
6. If the payment remains unresolved after 48 hours, escalate to the accounting team.

### Incorrect invoice

1. Pull the invoice and identify the specific discrepancy (wrong amount, wrong line items, wrong client, etc.).
2. Cross-reference the corresponding work order(s) to confirm what was actually delivered.
3. Determine whether the error requires a corrected invoice or a credit/adjustment.
4. Flag the invoice for correction by the accounting team with a clear description of the error.
5. Do not request payment on the incorrect invoice until it is corrected.
6. Notify the client that the invoice is under review and provide an estimated resolution timeline.

### Disputed charge

1. Pull the invoice and the related work order(s) to understand what was billed and why.
2. Review any notes, photos, or sign-in logs attached to the work order for supporting documentation.
3. Respond to the client with a clear explanation of the charge and the supporting evidence.
4. If the dispute is valid (e.g., work was not completed as described), escalate to accounting and operations for a credit or adjustment.
5. Log the dispute on the invoice record with outcome notes.

---

## 3. Client deliverable issue

### Deliverable not sent / late

1. Identify what deliverable was expected (report, document, photos, invoice, etc.) and when it was due.
2. Check the work order and project in the portal to locate the deliverable or understand why it wasn't sent.
3. If the deliverable exists in the system, send it to the client immediately with an apology for the delay.
4. If the deliverable is missing or incomplete, contact the responsible team member to expedite completion.
5. Set a hard deadline for delivery and communicate it to the client.
6. Log the delay and root cause in the project or work order notes for future follow-up.

### Wrong deliverable sent

1. Confirm with the client what they received versus what they expected.
2. Locate the correct deliverable in the portal or request it from the responsible team.
3. Send the correct deliverable to the client as soon as possible.
4. If sensitive information was accidentally sent to the wrong recipient, escalate immediately to management and follow any applicable data handling procedures.
5. Log the error and corrective action in the project notes.

### Client deliverable quality dispute

1. Pull the work order and any attached photos, notes, or sign-off documentation.
2. Review the scope of work agreed upon for this job.
3. If the deliverable meets the agreed scope, explain this to the client clearly using the supporting documentation.
4. If there is a legitimate quality shortfall, acknowledge it and propose a remediation plan (e.g., return visit, partial credit, replacement deliverable).
5. Escalate to the operations manager if the client is not satisfied after the initial response.
6. Document the dispute and resolution in the work order or project notes.
